I am currently working as a PostDoc in the field of visualization research at University of Bamberg, Germany. My research interests include **data visualization**, **game analytics**, and **artificial intelligence (AI)**. I explore and invent ways to analyze complex data using effective visual encodings, enabling exploration of patterns, and discovering valuable insights. I often apply these visualizations in practical use-cases scenarios, including those related to machine learning. For instance, understanding what AI models have learned. I have worked with experts in different communities, e.g., in *AICrowd* for crowdsourced AI development, *Pommerman* for analyzing AI agent gameplay behavior, etc. The visualization contributions were received well and awarded by the communities.

My mission is to advance the state-of-the-art in visual analytics and to create impactful solutions that can help various domains and applications. I collaborate with researchers and practitioners from different disciplines and backgrounds, and I enjoy sharing my knowledge and insights through teaching and mentoring. I am passionate about finding new problems, generating new ideas, and delivering high-quality results. 

I am happy to chat, connect, and collaborate. Please feel free to write me an e-mail: shivam[dot]agarwal[at_the_rate]uni-bamberg[dot]de

**Keywords**: exploratory data analysis, information visualization, visual analytics, dynamic set/graph visualization, artificial intelligence (AI), and game analytics.
